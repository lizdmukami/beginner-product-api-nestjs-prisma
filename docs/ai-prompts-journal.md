# 🤖 AI Prompts Journal

This document captures how I used AI throughout the project. Not just to get answers, but to guide my learning process, especially in areas I wasn’t fully confident in yet.

Instead of relying on AI to generate full solutions, I used it to:

- Break down concepts  
- Suggest next steps  
- Help debug errors  
- Validate my thinking  

---

## 📌 How I Approached Using AI

At the start, I noticed something quickly. If I asked vague questions, I got generic answers.  
But when I asked more specific, structured prompts, the responses became much more useful.

Over time, my prompts improved. And that made a noticeable difference.

---

## 🔍 Prompt 1: Understanding Relational Databases

**Prompt used:**
Explain relational databases in simple terms for a beginner working on a product and category system


**Why I used it**

Before building anything, I needed to understand how data would relate. Especially how products connect to categories.

**What AI helped with**

- Explained tables, rows, and relationships in a simple way  
- Introduced the idea of foreign keys  
- Helped visualize how Product → Category works  

**My evaluation**

This was a strong starting point. It simplified something that initially felt technical and abstract.

## 🧠 Reflection on AI Usage

At the beginning, I expected AI to just “give answers.”  
But over time, I realized something.

The real value wasn’t in the answers.  
It was in how it helped me think.

---

### What worked well

- Breaking down complex concepts  
- Debugging errors quickly  
- Giving structured guidance  

---

### What didn’t always work

- Sometimes responses were too generic  
- Occasionally needed to rephrase prompts  
- Not all suggestions worked immediately  

---

## 🧠 What I Learned About Prompting

### Good prompts:

- Are specific  
- Include context  
- Ask one clear question at a time  

### Bad prompts:

- Are too broad  
- Assume AI understands your exact problem without details  

---

## 💡 Final Takeaway

AI didn’t replace learning.  
It made learning faster and more guided.

Instead of being stuck for hours, I could:

- Ask  
- Try  
- Fail  
- Adjust  

And move forward.
